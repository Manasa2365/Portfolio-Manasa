Assignment 1: Geodesic Sphere

Introduction:

A geodesic sphere is a structure composed of hexagons and pentagons arranged in a spherical pattern. These structures are widely used in architecture, engineering, and material science due to their strength, lightweight properties, and efficient distribution of stress.

This project focuses on designing and assembling a 50mm diameter geodesic sphere using Fusion 360. The goal is to understand the geometric arrangement of hexagons and pentagons and effectively apply assembly techniques in Fusion 360.

Design Methodology:

Geometric Considerations

  • Diameter: 50mm
  • Edge length: 19.5mm
  • Primary Shapes: 20 Hexagons and 12 Pentagons
  • Layer-wise arrangement: The sphere is built layer by layer with precise angles.

Steps in Fusion 360:

Step 1: Creating the Base Layer

  • Sketch a hexagon with an edge length of 19.5mm.
  • Add a pentagon adjacent to it.
  • Use patterns or copies to begin the base layer.

Step 2: Layer-by-Layer Construction

  • Each layer is tilted from -90° onward.
  • Duplicate shapes and adjust orientation using the move and rotate tools.
  • Use joints to fix shapes together.

Step 3: Mirroring and Completing the Sphere

  • Mirror half the structure for symmetry.
  • Join mirrored parts edge-to-edge.

Step 4: Final Join

  • Use Align and Joint to connect final parts.
  • Apply the Combine tool for a unified body.

Challenges and Solutions:

Aligning Faces

Solution: Used the Align tool with face references.

Symmetry

Solution: Used Mirror and Pattern tools.

Fitting Angles

Solution: Used Rotate tool and layer logic.

Conclusion:

This project demonstrated 3D design principles using Fusion 360. Key learning includes geometric modeling, parametric control, and assembly accuracy. The geodesic sphere shows how digital tools can model real-world engineering objects.
